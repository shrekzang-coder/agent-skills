# Account Warming & Posting Cadence

Xiaohongshu's risk model tracks behavioral patterns, not just content. A freshly-posted note from an account that has been silent for weeks is more likely to be under-distributed than one from an account with recent organic activity.

## Before posting (web-end first post)

If the account has only been used on mobile until now, the creator web endpoint may trigger an extra device/location verification on the first post.

Recommended warmup (15 min before publishing):
1. Manually browse 10-15 notes on `www.xiaohongshu.com` in the same Chrome profile
2. Like 2-3 notes in the same niche as your upcoming post
3. Follow 1-2 creators in the niche
4. Leave at least one comment on an unrelated, active note

This creates a realistic "session before publish" trail.

## Posting windows

Higher-risk off-peak hours for automation heuristics:
- 02:00 - 07:00 (near-zero human traffic)
- 14:00 - 17:00 (mid-afternoon lull)

Safer windows (real user density is highest):
- 12:00 - 13:30 (lunch)
- 19:00 - 22:00 (evening)
- Weekends any time above

Publish at a natural time, not at :00 on the dot. `20:13` looks more human than `20:00`.

## Cadence

- First 7 days: at most 1 image-text note per day
- Never publish back-to-back within 10 minutes
- Mix content types after week 1 (image-text, short video, long article) — XHS rewards content-type variety on creator accounts
- If your first post underperforms, don't "make up" with a burst the same day — it looks like panic-farming

## After publishing

Within the first hour:
1. Like your own post (2 min after publishing, not immediately)
2. Reply to any comments within 5-15 minutes — reply rate in the first hour is an algorithmic signal
3. Leave 10-15 minutes of organic browsing in the same tab (scroll feed, like, enter a few notes)

Do NOT:
- Open creator.xiaohongshu.com, publish, and close the tab immediately
- Repost the same note to multiple accounts in parallel
- Use the same hashtag set verbatim across multiple posts in the same day

## Red flags that routinely trigger reduced distribution

- Uniform inter-action timing (every 1.0s clicks → bot signal)
- Same device publishing from different IPs within minutes
- Rapid topic list reuse across notes
- Sudden shift in posting hours (e.g. always 20:00 then suddenly 03:00)
- Comments that repeat brand mentions across unrelated posts → flagged as marketing/导流

## On commenting under others' posts

Commenting to promote your own brand/product on other creators' notes is the fastest way to get a "营销/导流" label. One or two contextual comments per week in the same niche is fine; 5+ comments with brand keywords per day is not.

Tone matters: pure endorsement of your own tool reads as water army. Reactions like "我也在用 X 踩过这个坑" read as peer commentary.
